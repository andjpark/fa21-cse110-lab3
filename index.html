Follow up thoughts from meeting 1:

As software engineers, we should strive to provide the best and most helpful experience to end users, rather than simply focusing on getting our product to work.
Of course, we need our code to work, but coding should be the least of our worries; instead, we should provide a seemless experience. We code for humans, not for computers.

Working in a strictly team environment, we cannot ignore our drastically different personalities and habits. Let's not assume that every other programmer is like us.
We're all human, striving towards the same goals, so let's facilitate that.

Summarized thoughts from meeting 2:

For the best team working experience, we cannot only look to build our relationships and connections in the workplace, but outside of work, too. Facilitating a healthy
dynamic between each other outside of work is keen to building strong team chemistry in order to better our workflow.

Let's start thinking of each other less as coworkers, and more as teammates and humans.

Unanswered questions and comments:

  • What if I simply do not get along with someone assigned to my team? What is the workaround our inherent incompatibility?
  • Would a strong friendship between two "teammates" be detrimental to the work produced?
  • If our lives and dynamics with each other are grounded upon the work we do, how can we ignore these boundaries when trying to establish a personal connection?
Food for Thought To be a good software engineer, you must first be a good human.
